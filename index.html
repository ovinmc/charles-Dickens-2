Charles Dickens

Charles John Huffam Dickens was an English writer and social critic. He created some of the world's best-known fictional characters and is regarded by many as the greatest novelist of the Victorian era. By the 20th century, critics and scholars had recognised him as a literary genius. His novels and short stories are widely read today. He left the school when he was the age of 12. His first novel was the Picnic Papers and the second was the Oliver Twist which has gone famous around the world during the victorian era and the present day too. He's most famous book was the Black house book.
